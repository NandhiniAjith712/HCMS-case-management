# Special Cases

HCMS supports special reporting modes for cases that need extra privacy or protection.

## Confidential cases

A confidential case restricts who can view it. Only authorized users at the matching escalation level, the case creator, and the current assignee can see it. Confidential cases are used when the employee does not want the details widely visible.

## Sensitive cases

A sensitive case is similar to a confidential case. Visibility is controlled by escalation level permissions. It is used for matters that require careful handling and limited access.

## Anonymous cases

An anonymous case hides the employee's identity from other users. The system still records the creator internally for access control, but the display name is masked as "Anonymous Employee". Anonymous cases are used when the employee wants to report an issue without revealing their identity.

## Important rules

- Employees can always see their own cases, including confidential, sensitive, and anonymous ones.
- Other users need explicit permission to view special cases.
- The employee should choose the reporting mode carefully when creating the case. It may not be possible to change the reporting mode later.
- Special cases may follow different escalation rules and visibility restrictions.
