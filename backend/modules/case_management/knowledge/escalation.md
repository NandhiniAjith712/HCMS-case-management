# Escalation Process

HCMS uses a level-based escalation system from L1 to L5.

## How escalation works

- A case starts at escalation level L1.
- If HR cannot resolve the case at the current level, it can be escalated to the next level (L2, L3, L4, or L5).
- Each level may have different users or departments assigned to handle it.
- For some escalations, the employee may be asked to give consent before the case is escalated.

## Escalation consent

When an escalation requires consent, the employee receives a notification. The employee can approve or reject the escalation. The case history records the consent response.

## Why cases are escalated

- The case needs a higher authority to make a decision.
- The assigned HR cannot resolve the issue within the SLA time.
- The case involves sensitive matters that need specialized handling.
- The employee explicitly requests escalation.

## What the employee can do

- Employees can view the escalation history of their own cases.
- Employees can respond to escalation consent requests.
- Employees cannot directly escalate a case unless the system provides the option on the case detail page.
